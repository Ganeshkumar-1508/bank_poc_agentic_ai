REGULATORY COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS FOR LENDING OPERATIONS
===========================================================
Document Reference: RCR-2024-003
Effective Date: January 2024
Version: 1.5
Department: Legal & Compliance

1. FEDERAL LENDING REGULATIONS OVERVIEW
========================================

1.1 Truth in Lending Act (TILA) / Regulation Z
-----------------------------------------------
All consumer loan disclosures must comply with TILA requirements:

Mandatory Disclosures:
  - Annual Percentage Rate (APR) must be clearly stated
  - Finance charges must be itemized
  - Total payment amount over the life of the loan
  - Payment schedule with due dates and amounts
  - Total interest payable
  - Late payment fees and penalties
  - Prepayment penalties (if applicable)
  - Security interest details

Timing Requirements:
  - Truth in Lending disclosure must be provided at least 3 business days before loan closing
  - Right of Rescission: 3 business days for refinances on primary residence
  - APR changes exceeding 0.125% require a new disclosure

1.2 Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) / Regulation B
--------------------------------------------------------
Prohibited Factors in Credit Decisions:
  - Race, color, religion, national origin
  - Sex, marital status, age (provided applicant has capacity to contract)
  - Receipt of public assistance income
  - Exercise of rights under the Consumer Credit Protection Act

Adverse Action Notice Requirements:
  - Must be provided within 30 days of the adverse action
  - Must include specific reasons for denial
  - Must include the applicant's credit score and score range
  - Must identify the credit bureau that provided the report
  - Must inform the applicant of their right to obtain a free credit report

Record Retention:
  - All loan applications must be retained for 25 months
  - Adverse action notices must be retained for 25 months
  - All supporting documentation must be retained for the life of the loan plus 5 years

1.3 Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
--------------------------------------
Permissible Purpose Requirements:
  - Credit reports may only be obtained with written authorization from the applicant
  - Permissible purposes include: credit application review, collection, employment, insurance underwriting

Consumer Rights:
  - Right to one free credit report per year from each bureau
  - Right to dispute inaccurate information
  - Right to limit prescreened offers
  - Right to seek damages for willful FCRA violations

Furnisher Requirements:
  - Must report accurate information to credit bureaus
  - Must investigate disputes within 30 days
  - Must notify consumers when negative information is reported
  - Must correct or delete inaccurate information promptly

1.4 Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA) / Regulation C
---------------------------------------------------------
HMDA Reporting Requirements:
  - All mortgage applications must be reported regardless of outcome
  - Data fields include: loan type, purpose, amount, property type, occupancy, applicant demographics, action taken, pricing information
  - Reports due annually by March 1st for the preceding calendar year
  - Data submitted via the HMDA Platform (LAR formatting)

Prohibited Practices:
  - Steering borrowers to specific loan products based on protected characteristics
  - Redlining or geographic discrimination
  - Discriminatory pricing based on protected characteristics

2. ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) REQUIREMENTS
=============================================

2.1 Customer Identification Program (CIP)
------------------------------------------
Required Information for All Loan Applicants:
  - Full legal name
  - Date of birth
  - Residential address (no P.O. boxes for primary residence loans)
  - Identification number (SSN for US persons, passport number for non-US persons)
  - Government-issued photo identification

Verification Procedures:
  - At least one document-based verification (driver's license, passport, state ID)
  - At least one non-document-based verification (credit bureau cross-reference, database check)
  - Enhanced verification for applicants flagged by AML screening systems

2.2 Suspicious Activity Monitoring
------------------------------------
Transactions That Must Be Flagged:
  - Loan proceeds transferred to high-risk jurisdictions
  - Multiple loan applications within 90 days
  - Applicants with unverifiable income sources
  - Cash deposits exceeding $10,000 related to loan transactions
  - Applicants on OFAC SDN list or other sanctions lists
  - Structured deposits designed to evade reporting thresholds

Suspicious Activity Reports (SAR):
  - Must be filed within 30 days of detection
  - Filed via FinCEN BSA E-Filing system
  - Confidentiality maintained - cannot notify the subject
  - Minimum filing threshold: Suspicious activity involving $5,000 or more

2.3 Currency Transaction Reporting (CTR)
-----------------------------------------
- CTR required for all cash transactions exceeding $10,000 in a single business day
- Multiple cash transactions totaling over $10,000 must be aggregated and reported
- CTR filed via FinCEN BSA E-Filing within 15 calendar days

3. INTEREST RATE AND FEE REGULATIONS
======================================

3.1 Usury Laws by State
-------------------------
Interest rate caps vary by state. The following are maximum allowable APRs:

No Usury Limit States: Delaware, South Dakota, Nevada (for nationally chartered banks)
Strict Usury States:
  - Arkansas: 17% (consumer loans)
  - Connecticut: 12% (non-bank lenders)
  - West Virginia: 18% (consumer loans)
  - New York: 16% (civil usury), 25% (criminal usury)

3.2 Military Lending Act (MLA) Compliance
------------------------------------------
Special Protections for Active Duty Military:
  - Maximum APR: 36% (Military Annual Percentage Rate - MAPR)
  - MAPR includes: interest, fees, credit insurance premiums, debt cancellation fees, ancillary products
  - Prohibited: mandatory arbitration clauses, prepayment penalties, wage garnishment (except spousal/child support)
  - Required: MLA disclosures before loan consummation
  - Verification: Must check DMDC database to confirm active duty status

3.3 Dodd-Frank Act Requirements
---------------------------------
Qualified Mortgage (QM) Standards:
  - No excessive upfront points and fees (total must not exceed 3% of loan amount)
  - No loan terms exceeding 30 years
  - No negative amortization
  - No interest-only loans (with limited exceptions)
  - DTI must not exceed 43% (for general QM)
  - Must verify income using IRS Form 4506-T or equivalent

Ability-to-Repay (ATR) Requirements:
  - Lender must make reasonable good faith determination of borrower's ability to repay
  - Must consider: income, assets, employment, current debt obligations, credit history
  - Must verify income and assets through reliable documentation
  - Total monthly debt payments must not exceed reasonable percentage of verified income

4. DATA PRIVACY AND SECURITY
=============================

4.1 Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)
-----------------------------------
Privacy Notice Requirements:
  - Initial privacy notice provided at the start of the customer relationship
  - Annual privacy notice sent to all customers
  - Opt-out notice for sharing nonpublic personal information with non-affiliated third parties
  - Notice must describe: information collected, how information is shared, how information is protected

Safeguards Rule:
  - Written information security plan required
  - Designation of employee(s) to coordinate the information security program
  - Risk assessment of potential threats to customer information
  - Regular testing of safeguards
  - Service provider oversight and contractual requirements

4.2 State Privacy Laws
-----------------------
California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):
  - Right to know what personal information is collected
  - Right to delete personal information
  - Right to opt-out of the sale of personal information
  - Right to non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights

4.3 Data Breach Notification
------------------------------
- Notification to affected individuals within 60 days of discovery
- Notification to state attorneys general for breaches affecting 500+ residents
- Notification to federal regulators as required by breach severity
- Document all breach response actions and remediation efforts

5. CONSUMER PROTECTION REQUIREMENTS
=====================================

5.1 Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA)
---------------------------------------------
- Prior express written consent required for auto-dialed calls and texts
- Do-Not-Call list compliance mandatory
- Time restrictions: Calls only between 8 AM and 9 PM local time
- Mobile numbers require express consent regardless of relationship

5.2 Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act (ESIGN)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
- Electronic signatures are legally valid for loan documents
- Consumer must consent to electronic delivery before signing
- Consumer must be informed of their right to receive paper copies
- System must maintain audit trail of all electronic signatures

5.3 Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA)
-------------------------------------------
Protections for Active Military:
  - Interest rate cap: 6% on pre-service obligations (mortgages, auto loans, credit cards)
  - Protection from eviction: For rent up to a certain threshold
  - Right to terminate leases upon deployment or PCS orders
  - Stay of proceedings: Civil court proceedings must be stayed during active service
  - Must verify military status using DMDC database
  - Benefits apply from the date of active duty notification

6. EXAMINATION AND AUDIT REQUIREMENTS
======================================

6.1 Internal Audit Schedule
-----------------------------
- Quarterly: Loan file sampling review (minimum 5% of originated loans)
- Semi-Annual: Compliance with TILA, ECOA, FCRA, HMDA
- Annual: Full AML compliance review and BSA risk assessment
- Annual: Fair lending analysis including disparate impact testing

6.2 Regulatory Examination Readiness
--------------------------------------
Files That Must Be Maintained and Current:
  - Written policies and procedures for all compliance areas
  - Board-approved compliance program charter
  - Training records for all lending staff (annual certification required)
  - Sample loan files demonstrating compliance
  - Complaint log and resolution tracking
  - HMDA LAR data with quality control reviews
  - BSA/AML risk assessment and SAR filing records
