INTERNAL COMPLIANCE STANDARDS AND OPERATIONAL PROCEDURES
===========================================================
Document Reference: ICS-2024-005
Effective Date: January 2024
Version: 1.8
Department: Internal Audit & Compliance

1. LOAN APPROVAL AUTHORITY AND ESCALATION HIERARCHY
======================================================

1.1 Approval Authority Levels
------------------------------
Level 1 - Automated Approval (System):
  - Risk Grade A and B
  - Loan amount up to $25,000
  - All eligibility criteria met
  - No exceptions or overrides required
  - No automatic processing: Loan officer review within 1 business day

Level 2 - Loan Officer Approval:
  - Risk Grade C
  - Loan amount $25,000 - $75,000
  - Minor exceptions documented (within policy tolerance)
  - Must document rationale in loan file
  - Processing time: Within 2 business days

Level 3 - Branch Manager Approval:
  - Risk Grade D
  - Loan amount $75,000 - $250,000
  - Any exception to standard policy
  - Requires documented compensating factors
  - Processing time: Within 3 business days

Level 4 - Credit Committee Approval:
  - Risk Grade E
  - Loan amount $250,000 - $1,000,000
  - Multiple policy exceptions
  - Non-standard loan structures
  - Meeting: Weekly on Tuesdays
  - Processing time: Within 5 business days

Level 5 - Executive Approval:
  - Risk Grade F (exceptions only)
  - Loan amount exceeding $1,000,000
  - Policy exceptions exceeding tolerance thresholds
  - Requires CEO or CFO signature
  - Board notification required within 5 business days

1.2 Mandatory Escalation Triggers
-----------------------------------
The following situations require mandatory escalation regardless of risk grade:
  - Applicant is an employee or employee's immediate family member
  - Application involves a politically exposed person (PEP)
  - Fraud indicators detected at any stage
  - Application value exceeds 5% of total portfolio
  - Adverse media search returns negative results
  - Applicant has existing non-performing loan
  - Sanctions screening generates potential match

2. LOAN DOCUMENTATION STANDARDS
=================================

2.1 Complete File Requirements
-------------------------------
Every approved loan file MUST contain the following documents:

  - Signed and dated loan application
  - Identity verification documents (all borrowers)
  - Income verification (all borrowers)
  - Credit report with scores
  - Credit decision documentation (approval/decline letter)
  - Risk assessment worksheet (if manually underwritten)
  - Appraisal or valuation (for secured loans)
  - Collateral documentation (title, lien filing, UCC)
  - Insurance verification (for secured loans)
  - Signed promissory note
  - Signed disclosure statements (TILA, ECOA, etc.)
  - AML/KYC verification checklist
  - OFAC screening results

2.2 Quality Control Sampling
------------------------------
Pre-Funding QC (10% random sample):
  - Verify income documentation matches application
  - Verify property appraisal independence
  - Verify DTI calculation accuracy
  - Verify all required disclosures provided timely
  - Verify collateral valuation reasonableness
  - Must be completed before loan disbursement

Post-Closing QC (10% random sample within 90 days):
  - Verify all documents executed correctly
  - Verify lien perfection (for secured loans)
  - Verify insurance coverage adequacy
  - Verify rate and fee accuracy vs disclosures
  - Verify HMDA data accuracy

3. FAIR LENDING COMPLIANCE
============================

3.1 Prohibited Practices
--------------------------
The following practices are strictly prohibited:
  - Steering borrowers to specific loan products based on race, gender, age, or national origin
  - Offering different terms to similarly qualified borrowers based on protected characteristics
  - Setting different credit thresholds for different demographic groups
  - Redlining (denying or restricting loans based on neighborhood demographics)
  - Reverse redlining (targeting high-cost products to minority neighborhoods)
  - Discouraging applications from protected class members
  - Using proxy variables that correlate with protected characteristics (e.g., zip code as proxy for race)

3.2 Fair Lending Testing Program
----------------------------------
Annual fair lending testing must include:
  - Statistical analysis of approval rates by race, gender, age, and national origin
  - Disparate treatment testing using matched-pair methodology
  - Disparate impact analysis comparing outcomes across demographic groups
  - Pricing analysis for disparities in interest rates and fees
  - Geographic analysis to identify potential redlining patterns

3.3 Bias Detection in AI/ML Models
-------------------------------------
Machine learning models used in credit decisions must be monitored for:
  - Proxies for protected characteristics (e.g., zip code, school name, employer name)
  - Adverse impact ratio analysis (approval rate for protected group / approval rate for control group)
  - Threshold: 4/5ths rule - if any group's approval rate is less than 80% of the highest group, investigation required
  - Model explainability: Must be able to explain denial reasons in plain language
  - Annual third-party bias audit required for all production models

4. INTEREST RATE AND FEE COMPLIANCE
====================================

4.1 Rate Ceiling Compliance
-----------------------------
  - All loan APRs must comply with applicable state usury laws
  - Rate locks must be honored for minimum 60 days (mortgage), 30 days (other loans)
  - If rate increases after lock, borrower must be notified within 3 business days
  - No bait-and-switch: Advertised rate must be available to reasonably qualified applicants

4.2 Fee Transparency Requirements
-----------------------------------
  - All fees must be disclosed before loan closing
  - Origination fee maximum: 5% for loans up to $20,000, 3% for loans above $20,000
  - Late payment fee maximum: $25 or 5% of past-due payment, whichever is less
  - No hidden fees: All charges must be itemized in the Truth in Lending disclosure
  - Prepayment penalty prohibited on all consumer loans

4.3 Rate Floor and Rate Spread Reporting
-----------------------------------------
  - APR spread over Treasury rate must be tracked for HOEPA compliance
  - HOEPA triggers: APR exceeds average prime offer rate by 8.5% (first lien) or 10% (subordinate lien)
  - Points and fees exceed 8% of total loan amount or $800 (whichever is greater)
  - HOEPA loans require additional disclosures and restrictions

5. DELINQUENCY AND COLLECTIONS COMPLIANCE
==========================================

5.1 Early Delinquency Management (1-30 Days Past Due)
-------------------------------------------------------
  - Day 1 past due: Automated reminder via SMS/email
  - Day 5 past due: Phone call from servicing team
  - Day 15 past due: Written notice via US mail
  - Day 30 past due: Formal demand letter with payment plan options

5.2 Collections Compliance Requirements
-----------------------------------------
  - All collections activity must comply with the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA)
  - Cannot call before 8 AM or after 9 PM local time
  - Cannot harass, oppress, or abuse the debtor
  - Cannot misrepresent the amount owed or legal consequences
  - Must honor cease-and-desist requests (may still file lawsuit)
  - Must validate debt within 5 days of initial contact
  - Cannot report inaccurate information to credit bureaus

5.3 Loss Mitigation Options
-----------------------------
Before proceeding to foreclosure or charge-off, the following loss mitigation options must be explored:
  - Loan modification (rate reduction, term extension, principal forbearance)
  - Forbearance agreement (temporary payment reduction)
  - Repayment plan (catch-up over 6-12 months)
  - Short sale (with borrower consent)
  - Deed-in-lieu of foreclosure (with borrower consent)

5.4 Charge-Off Standards
--------------------------
  - Consumer loans: Charge-off at 120 days past due
  - Mortgage loans: Charge-off at 180 days past due
  - All charge-offs require written approval from Level 3 or above
  - Charge-off recovery efforts continue for minimum 3 years

6. ANNUAL COMPLIANCE TRAINING REQUIREMENTS
============================================

6.1 Mandatory Training Topics
-------------------------------
All lending staff must complete annual training on:
  - Fair lending laws and prohibited practices (2 hours)
  - BSA/AML requirements and suspicious activity reporting (2 hours)
  - Truth in Lending Act disclosures (1 hour)
  - Identity theft prevention program (1 hour)
  - Cybersecurity and data privacy (1 hour)
  - Workplace harassment and ethics (1 hour)
  Total minimum: 8 hours annually

6.2 Role-Specific Training
----------------------------
  - Loan Officers: Additional 4 hours on underwriting standards and policy exceptions
  - Branch Managers: Additional 4 hours on approval authority and escalation procedures
  - Collections Staff: Additional 4 hours on FDCPA compliance and loss mitigation
  - Compliance Officers: Additional 8 hours on regulatory updates and examination preparation

6.3 Training Documentation
----------------------------
  - All training completion must be documented in the Learning Management System (LMS)
  - Non-completion by December 31 results in temporary suspension of lending authority
  - New hires must complete all mandatory training within 30 days of start date
  - Training records must be maintained for 5 years and available for regulatory examination
